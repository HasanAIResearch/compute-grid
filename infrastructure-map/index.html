Institutional frame

Demand is visible. Delivery is the constraint.

Institutional research increasingly frames AI as an infrastructure buildout: capex must pass through physical gates before it becomes revenue-producing capacity.

Planned capacity is not delivered capacity.

Permits, grid queues, transformers, labor, and equipment timing can separate announcements from energized sites.

Bottlenecks rotate.

When one layer improves, scarcity can move to memory, optics, switchgear, cooling, or construction capacity.

Beneficiaries are broader than GPUs.

The buildout reaches semiconductors, electrical equipment, utilities, power systems, cooling, data centers, and builders.
